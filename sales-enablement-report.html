Bottom Line

The team sees good effort and a hardworking product org, but enablement sessions are not yet translating to deal-ready confidence. Demo confidence scored lowest at 2.9 — 3 reps rated themselves a 2 out of 5. The most consistent piece of feedback: the team needs to click through things themselves before they can demo. Written materials and walkthroughs aren't enough.

The Hard Truth

Multiple respondents flagged that no prospect-facing features have shipped since September 2025. One rep noted the demo fund hasn't been updated since October because there's nothing new to show. This isn't just an enablement problem — it's a product velocity problem that's eroding sales confidence.

02

Enablement Effectiveness

The team rated overall enablement preparedness at 3.2/5. Ross's sessions are the clear bright spot. Everything else needs work.

What's Working
Ross's Sessions — The Gold Standard

Ross was mentioned by name in 4 of 9 responses as the most helpful part of enablement. The team values his storytelling approach, charisma, and ability to tie product features to real prospect conversations. Multiple reps specifically requested that future sessions coordinate with Ross.

Storytelling Over Slides

Ross's ability to weave product capabilities into real sales narratives resonates far more than feature walkthroughs.

PBOR Framework

The PBOR (Problem-Bridge-Outcome-Result) framework was cited twice as a useful structural tool for conversations.

Tying Features Together

The session connecting MXL, transaction register, and report builder into a cohesive story was specifically praised.

Root Cause

The pattern is clear: the team has been told about features but hasn't practiced using them. They've watched demos but haven't driven them. Without hands-on click-through time, confidence stays theoretical. Additionally, the demo environment itself was flagged as unreliable — reps can't practice if the environment doesn't work.

06

Action Plan

Concrete actions organized by urgency. These are derived directly from team feedback — not editorial suggestions.

Guiding Principle

The team doesn't need more information. They need tools to practice, stories to tell, and proof that the product is moving forward. Every action below should be evaluated against that bar.

This Week — Immediate Fixes

Target: March 10–14, 2026

Demo Environment

Fix or rebuild the demo environment

Reps can't practice on a broken demo. Ensure the demo fund is current and functional. Owner: Product/Engineering.

Click-Through Paths

Create 3 guided demo scripts

Self-service click paths for: (1) Transaction Register, (2) Report Builder, (3) MXL overview. Written as step-by-step with screenshots. Owner: Product Marketing.

AI Content Audit

Review and rewrite flagged materials

Pull all white papers and product overviews. Rewrite anything that reads as generic AI output. Shorter, specific, human. Owner: Content/Product Marketing.

Next 2 Weeks — Core Materials

Target: March 14–28, 2026

Case Studies

Publish 2–3 customer case studies

This was the #1 request (9/9 reps). Focus on specific firm types with real outcomes. Include: challenge, approach, measurable result. Owner: Product Marketing + Customer Success.

Objection Handling

Create objection-response guide

Top 10 objections the team hears, with recommended responses. Sourced from the team directly. Owner: Sales Leadership + Product Marketing.

Talk Tracks

Feature-level talk tracks

One-page talk tracks for: Transaction Register, Report Builder, MXL, and Distribution Timing. Include: what it does, who cares, how to position. Owner: Product Marketing, coordinating with Ross.

Next 30 Days — Structural Changes

Target: by April 9, 2026

Ride-Alongs

Product joins 2+ sales calls per week

3 reps asked for this. Product should join real prospect/customer calls to hear firsthand what the market is asking. Owner: Product Leadership.

Roadmap Transparency

Share quarterly product roadmap with sales

The team needs to know what's coming and when. Even high-level timelines restore confidence. Owner: Product Leadership.

Ross Collaboration

Formalize Ross's role in enablement

The team's top-rated enablement resource is a person, not a document. Coordinate all future sessions with Ross for maximum AE buy-in. Owner: Sales Leadership + Product.

The Bigger Conversation

Several reps raised a concern that goes beyond enablement: the team hasn't seen a prospect-facing feature ship since September 2025. No amount of enablement materials can compensate for a product that isn't visibly evolving. This needs to be addressed at the product strategy level — and communicated transparently to the sales team when it is.
